Day 1 — Push / Serratus

Chest · Triceps · Serratus Anterior

PE-Safe · No Flat Bench 6 Exercises ~50 min
▶
free-exercise-db
Incline Dumbbell Press
4×10
🏋️ Dumbbells
🎯 Chest, Triceps
  1. Set bench to 30–45° incline — upper chest without shoulder impingement
  2. Retract scapula before pressing; keep them pinched throughout
  3. Lower dumbbells to chest line, not below — protect PE sternum
  4. Press in a slight arc, meet at top without fully locking elbows
  5. Control the eccentric — 3 seconds down
✓ PE-Safe: Incline angle reduces direct sternal pressure vs. flat bench
▶
free-exercise-db
Incline Cable Fly
3×12
🔗 Cable Machine
🎯 Upper Chest, Serratus
  1. Set cables at low position, bench at 30–45° incline
  2. At the top, actively reach and protract (push the scapulae apart) — this fires serratus anterior
  3. Wide arc on the descent, feel the chest stretch
  4. Control the return, never let cables yank arms back
  5. Light weight — this is serratus work, not ego lifting
✓ PE-Safe: Top protraction squeeze directly activates serratus anterior
▶
free-exercise-db
Low Cable Crossover
3×15
🔗 Cable Machine
🎯 Serratus, Upper Chest
  1. Pull cables from low to high in a hugging arc
  2. At peak contraction, CRUNCH your scapulae apart (protract) — this IS the serratus move
  3. Slight forward lean for full range of motion
  4. Pause 1 second at top, feel ribs wing forward
  5. 3 sets lightweight — serratus activation, not load
✓ PE-Safe: Low-to-high arc avoids compression on the sternum
▶
free-exercise-db
Chest Dips
3×8–12
⚡ Parallel Bars
🎯 Lower Chest, Triceps
  1. Lean forward 20–30° to shift emphasis to chest
  2. Lower until upper arm is parallel to ground — don't go deeper if shoulders protest
  3. Keep elbows flared at ~45° angle
  4. Press back up, do NOT fully lock out at top
  5. Skip or use assisted machine if shoulder pain occurs
✓ PE-Safe: No sternal contact; forward lean keeps ribs clear
▶
free-exercise-db
Cable Rope Overhead Tricep Extension
3×12
🔗 Cable + Rope
🎯 Long Head Tricep
  1. Face away from cable stack, rope pulled overhead
  2. Elbows point straight up — don't let them flare wide
  3. Lower rope behind head with full stretch (feel long head)
  4. Extend fully to lockout, squeeze at top
  5. Core braced throughout — resist torso lean
▶
free-exercise-db
Push-Up to Side Plank
3×8/side
⚡ Bodyweight
🎯 Serratus, Core, Chest
  1. Perform a push-up — at the top, PUSH the floor away (serratus activation)
  2. Rotate into side plank, reach free arm to ceiling
  3. Hold side plank for 1–2 sec — feel under-arm ribs tighten
  4. Return to push-up, alternate sides
  5. If too hard: do the push-up + rotation without the top press
✓ Serratus Key: The "plus" push-up (extra protraction at top) is the most direct serratus exercise

Day 2 — Pull / Rhomboid

Lats · Mid-Back · Rhomboids · Lower Traps

Rhomboid Rehab Focus 5 Exercises ~45 min
▶
free-exercise-db
Wide-Grip Lat Pulldown
4×10
🔗 Cable Machine
🎯 Lats, Rhomboids
  1. Slight backward lean (~15°), chest up, proud posture
  2. Initiate pull by DEPRESSING scapulae first (pull shoulders down before arms move)
  3. Drive elbows toward hips — not behind you
  4. Touch bar to upper chest, squeeze lats at bottom
  5. Control the ascent — resist the bar going up
✓ Rhomboid: Scapular depression initiation hits rhomboids at stretch, lower traps on contraction
▶
free-exercise-db
Seated Cable Row
4×10
🔗 Cable Machine
🎯 Mid-Back, Rhomboids
  1. Use a wide/overhand grip to increase rhomboid recruitment
  2. Row to lower sternum/upper abs — elbows flare slightly out
  3. At peak contraction: squeeze shoulder blades TOGETHER and DOWN (retraction + depression)
  4. Hold the contraction 1–2 seconds — this is the rhomboid squeeze
  5. Let the shoulder blades FULLY protract on return — full range = full rehab
✓ Rhomboid Rehab: Wide grip + elbow flare = maximal rhomboid/mid-trap activation
▶
free-exercise-db
Face Pull
4×15
🔗 Cable + Rope
🎯 Rear Delt, Lower Trap, Rhomboid
  1. Cable set at face height; use rope attachment with overhand grip
  2. Pull rope TO FACE — separate hands at end of movement (thumbs toward ears)
  3. Elbows must stay at or ABOVE shoulder height throughout
  4. Think: "show off your biceps and squeeze your rhomboids"
  5. Light weight only — this is corrective work, not heavy pulling
✓ Rhomboid Rehab: Arguably the #1 corrective exercise for rhomboid weakness and rounded shoulders
▶
free-exercise-db
One-Arm Dumbbell Row
3×10/side
🏋️ Dumbbell
🎯 Lats, Mid-Back, Rhomboids
  1. Neutral spine, knee and hand on bench for support
  2. Initiate with the ELBOW, not the hand — pull elbow to hip
  3. At top: rotate torso slightly and really squeeze the shoulder blade in
  4. Full protraction on the descent — shoulder blade reaches forward
  5. Don't rush — this is a 2-second up, 3-second down movement
▶
free-exercise-db
Reverse Fly (Y-Raise Focus)
4×15
🏋️ Light Dumbbells
🎯 Lower Trap, Rhomboid, Rear Delt
  1. Prone on incline bench (45°) or bent over; chest supported is ideal
  2. Raise arms in a Y-shape (30–45° above horizontal) — NOT straight out like a T
  3. Thumbs pointing UP at the top — this externally rotates and fires the lower traps
  4. Squeeze at the top for 2 seconds — you should feel mid-back below the upper traps
  5. VERY light weight — lower trap and rhomboids fatigue quickly on these
✓ Rhomboid Key: Y-raise with thumb-up cue = lower trap + rhomboid maximal activation. The most targeted rehab move for this area.

Day 3 — Legs + Core

Quads · Hamstrings · Glutes · Abs

Compound Foundation 6 Exercises ~55 min
▶
free-exercise-db
Goblet Squat
4×12
🏋️ Dumbbell / Kettlebell
🎯 Quads, Glutes, Core
  1. Hold weight at chest height with both hands (goblet position)
  2. Feet shoulder-width, toes out 15–30°
  3. Sit BETWEEN your heels, not back — elbows should track inside knees at the bottom
  4. Chest stays up — weight acts as counterbalance
  5. Drive through full foot, not just heels; squeeze glutes at top
▶
free-exercise-db
Leg Press
4×12
⚡ Leg Press Machine
🎯 Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes
  1. Feet hip-width in the middle of the platform
  2. Lower until knees are at 90° — back stays flat against pad
  3. NEVER let lower back round off the seat — this is a spine injury
  4. Do NOT lock out knees at the top — keep slight bend
  5. For glute emphasis: higher foot placement; quad emphasis: lower
▶
free-exercise-db
Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
3×10
🏋️ Barbell or Dumbbells
🎯 Hamstrings, Glutes, Lower Back
  1. Hip-hinge movement — push hips BACK, not down
  2. Bar/dumbbells stay close to legs throughout — drag them down your thighs
  3. Maintain neutral spine at all times — no rounding the lower back
  4. Feel the hamstrings stretch as you hinge; that is your depth cue, not the floor
  5. Drive hips through at the top — full glute squeeze
▶
free-exercise-db
Walking Lunge
3×12/leg
⚡ Bodyweight / Dumbbells
🎯 Quads, Glutes, Hip Flexors
  1. Step forward far enough that both knees form 90° angles
  2. Back knee barely touches (or hovers above) the floor
  3. Torso stays upright — don't lean forward
  4. Drive through front heel to stand; step forward with back foot
  5. Add dumbbells once bodyweight feels easy for 3×15+
▶
free-exercise-db
Lying Leg Curls
3×12
⚡ Leg Curl Machine
🎯 Hamstrings (Bicep Femoris)
  1. Adjust pad to just above ankles — not on calves
  2. Curl heels toward glutes — full range of motion
  3. Keep hips pressed into the pad — no lifting the butt
  4. Squeeze hamstrings hard at peak contraction; hold 1 sec
  5. Slow eccentric (3 sec) — this is where the growth happens
▶
free-exercise-db
Plank
3×45–90 sec
⚡ Bodyweight
🎯 Core, Serratus, Shoulders
  1. Forearms and toes on ground; elbows under shoulders
  2. PUSH floor away from you — this engages serratus and prevents collapse
  3. Squeeze glutes and quads; don't let hips sag or rise
  4. Neutral neck — don't crane up or drop head
  5. Breathe steadily; brace core like you're bracing for a punch
✓ Serratus bonus: Active push-away cue fires serratus anterior, reinforcing Day 1 work

Day 4 — Shoulders

Anterior · Lateral · Posterior Deltoids

5 Exercises ~45 min Rear Delt Emphasis
▶
free-exercise-db
Arnold Press
4×10
🏋️ Dumbbells
🎯 All 3 Delt Heads
  1. Start with palms facing you (like top of a curl)
  2. As you press, rotate palms OUT — forward-facing at the top
  3. The rotation recruits all three delt heads in sequence
  4. Don't rush the rotation — it should be smooth and controlled
  5. Slight backward lean; don't press directly overhead (protect shoulder)
▶
free-exercise-db
Side Lateral Raise
4×15
🏋️ Light Dumbbells
🎯 Lateral Delt (Width)
  1. Slight forward lean of the torso (10–15°) — maximizes lateral delt
  2. Lead with your ELBOWS, not your hands — think "pour a pitcher of water"
  3. Stop at shoulder height — going higher turns it into a trap exercise
  4. Slight internal rotation (pinky slightly higher) targets lateral delt best
  5. Go light — lateral delts are small. 4 sets of 15 with perfect form > ego weight
▶
free-exercise-db
Lying Rear Lateral Raise
3×15
🏋️ Light Dumbbells
🎯 Rear Delt, Lower Trap, Rhomboid
  1. Lie prone on flat or slight incline bench, face down
  2. Arms hang neutral or slightly in front of shoulders
  3. Raise arms in a Y or T pattern with THUMBS UP for external rotation
  4. Feel the rear delt/mid-back squeeze at the top — hold 1–2 sec
  5. Gravity-assisted isolation — very light weight, high reps
✓ Rhomboid bonus: Prone position gravity-loads the rhomboid and lower trap without spinal loading
▶
free-exercise-db
Dumbbell Shoulder Press
4×10
🏋️ Dumbbells
🎯 Front + Lateral Delt, Triceps
  1. Start at ear level, elbows at ~90° (slight forward tilt preferred over T-pose)
  2. Press UP and slightly together — meet at top without touching
  3. Don't fully lock out — keep tension on delts throughout
  4. Lower slowly (3 sec) — this is where shoulder stability is built
  5. Core braced, lower back slightly arched but natural — no excessive lean back
▶
free-exercise-db
Scapular Pull-Up
3×10–12
⚡ Pull-Up Bar
🎯 Rhomboids, Lower Trap, Serratus
  1. Hang from pull-up bar, arms straight, relaxed
  2. WITHOUT bending elbows, DEPRESS your shoulder blades (pull them down)
  3. You will rise ~2 inches — this is the entire movement
  4. Hold the contraction for 2–3 seconds; feel rhomboids and lower traps fire
  5. Slowly return to dead hang — full elevation
✓ Best shoulder stability exercise in existence. Directly strengthens the scapular stabilizers responsible for PE posture correction.

Mid-Back · Lats · Biceps (Long & Short Head)

4 Exercises ~40 min Arm Emphasis Day

Bent Over Barbell Row
4×8
🏋️ Barbell
🎯 Mid-Back, Lats, Rhomboids, Biceps
  1. Hinge to ~45°; overhand grip slightly wider than shoulder-width
  2. Row the bar to your LOWER chest / upper abdomen — not your belly button
  3. Lead with elbows, NOT hands — think "stab elbows behind you"
  4. Hold the contraction; retract and depress scapula simultaneously
  5. If lower back fatigues: switch to chest-supported incline row instead

🔗 Cable Machine
🎯 Lats (Long Head), Biceps
  1. Close neutral or underhand grip emphasizes lat long head and biceps
  2. Lean back slightly; pull bar to upper chest
  3. Think "elbows to pockets" — drives lats through full range
  4. Really squeeze at the bottom; don't just move the bar
  5. Full stretch at top — feel lats elongate

3×10
🏋️ EZ Curl Bar
🎯 Biceps Short + Long Head
  1. Semi-supinated grip (inner angled grip) — easier on wrists than straight bar
  2. Elbows stay pinned to sides throughout — no swinging or using momentum
  3. Full range: nearly full extension at bottom, full supination/contraction at top
  4. Squeeze the bicep at the top — don't just touch your shoulders and drop
  5. Slow eccentric (3 sec) — this builds real size

Hammer Curls
3×12
🏋️ Dumbbells
🎯 Brachialis, Brachioradialis, Bicep Long Head
  1. Neutral grip (thumbs up) throughout entire movement
  2. Curl alternating or simultaneously — alternate gives more control
  3. Hit the brachialis (under the bicep) — this gives arm THICKNESS
  4. Don't supinate at the top — keep neutral to target brachialis
  5. Superset with EZ-bar for a killer bicep pump

📋 Prep Strategy
🌅

Sunday Meal Prep (2 hours)

Cook 3 lbs chicken breast in bulk (bake at 400°F, 22 min). Make 6 cups rice. Wash and portion broccoli. Hard-boil 8 eggs. Divide into 5-day containers. This covers lunches and most dinners — no daily cooking required.

⚡

Pre/Post Workout Simplicity

Pre-workout: overnight oats (5 min the night before — oats + protein powder + almond milk in a jar). Post-workout: whey protein + dextrose (fast carbs) in a shaker — zero prep. Don't overthink the peri-workout window.

🎯

The 200g Protein Formula

Chicken breast (30g/4oz) · Salmon (35g/5oz) · Whey scoop (25g) · Casein scoop (30g) · Greek yogurt (17g/cup) · Eggs (6g each) · Cottage cheese (14g/half cup). Hit these daily sources and you'll never miss the target.

💧

Hydration Protocol

1 gallon water minimum on training days. Add electrolytes post-workout, especially in summer. Creatine (5g/day) can be added to post-workout shake — no loading phase needed, just consistent daily dosing.

🔄

Rest Day Adjustments

On rest days, drop carbs by ~50–75g (remove the intra-workout banana and reduce rice portions). Protein stays the same. Fat and vegetables can increase slightly to fill the calorie gap. Total rest day target: ~2,300 kcal.

🏗️ Program Design

This 5-day split is built around three priorities: maximizing muscle development using PE-safe movement patterns, correcting rhomboid weakness through targeted rehab, and activating the serratus anterior to improve posture and shoulder girdle stability.

Why PE-Safe Matters: Pectus excavatum creates a concave sternum that makes flat barbell bench press uncomfortable and potentially unsafe (increased sternal pressure). All pressing is done incline, which shifts load to the clavicular head of the pec while eliminating sternal contact.

🩺 Rhomboid Rehab Protocol

Weak rhomboids (the muscles between your shoulder blades) are extremely common and lead to rounded shoulders, neck pain, and poor posture. This program addresses them with a four-pronged approach:

  • Face Pulls (Day 2, 4×15): The gold-standard corrective for scapular retraction + external rotation
  • Y-Raises / Reverse Fly (Day 2, 4×15): Direct lower trap + rhomboid isolation with thumbs-up cue
  • Scapular Pull-Ups (Day 4, 3×12): Teaches proper scapular depression under load
  • Seated Cable Row Technique (Day 2): Full protraction → retraction ROM retrains scapular movement pattern

🫁 Serratus Anterior Activation

The serratus anterior ("boxer's muscle") protracts the scapula and holds the shoulder blade flat against the rib cage. In PE, this muscle is often inhibited due to anatomical constraints. Targeted activation:

  • Plus Push-Up / Push-Up to Side Plank (Day 1): The extra protraction at the top of a push-up is the most direct serratus exercise in existence
  • Incline Cable Fly (Day 1): Active protraction squeeze at the top of each rep
  • Low Cable Crossover (Day 1): High-arc protraction motion
  • Plank with Active Push (Day 3): Isometric serratus loading

📊 Progressive Overload

Follow this progression model to ensure continuous adaptation:

  1. Weeks 1–2: Learn the movements. Use conservative weights. Focus 100% on form cues.
  2. Weeks 3–4: Add 5–10 lbs to compounds weekly; 2.5 lbs to isolations when all reps feel easy
  3. Week 5: Deload — drop weight 40%, same reps. Let connective tissue recover.
  4. Repeat cycle: Strength builds in the recovery, not the workout. Deloads are not optional.
